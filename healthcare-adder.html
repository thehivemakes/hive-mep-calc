How This Works

Healthcare and senior living facilities require MEP systems that exceed standard commercial specifications. This tool itemizes each premium and shows the total cost impact above a commercial baseline.

Base MEP $/SF: The commercial office MEP cost per square foot — either from the ROM Estimator or from your own data. This is the "before" number.

Adders: Each line item represents a system or requirement that adds cost beyond the commercial baseline. Toggle items on/off, adjust the $/SF rate, and see the impact in real-time.

Key drivers of healthcare MEP premiums:

  • ASHRAE 170 ventilation: Healthcare requires significantly higher air change rates than ASHRAE 62.1 commercial standard. Patient rooms need 6+ ACH vs 4 ACH for offices. This drives larger ducts, bigger AHUs, more energy.
  • Medical gas: Piped oxygen, vacuum, medical air systems are unique to healthcare. Cost depends on outlet count and pipe routing.
  • Redundant power: Healthcare requires emergency generators, automatic transfer switches, and dual power distribution beyond standard commercial. NEC Article 517 and NFPA 110 govern requirements.
  • Nurse call / monitoring: Wired or wireless systems for patient communication. IP-based systems trending higher cost but lower installation labor.

Defaults: Mid-range estimates for Mid-Atlantic market. All values are editable — override with your project-specific data. Rates are per building SF unless noted otherwise.

Code Edition Notice: This tool references ASHRAE 170 (healthcare ventilation), NEC Article 517 (healthcare electrical), NFPA 99 (healthcare facilities code), and NFPA 110 (emergency power). These standards update on 3-year cycles (ASHRAE 170 on a 4-year cycle aligned with FGI). State and local jurisdictions adopt different editions at different times — adoption lag is typically 3-6 years. For example, CMS currently enforces NFPA 99-2012 despite the 2024 edition being current. Cost adder rates shown are planning-level estimates, not code-derived requirements. Always confirm applicable code editions and requirements with your Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ) and a licensed professional engineer before making design or procurement decisions.
